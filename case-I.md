# F-14 Tomcat: Case I Carrier Recovery Master Manual

### 1. The HSI "Ruler" (Spatial Awareness)
The HSI is your primary tool for setting up the "box." To avoid overshooting or dropping like a stone, you must manage your lateral distance (spacing).
* **The Vertical Line:** This represents the ship’s extended landing centerline.
* **The Outermost Hash Mark:** This is the second mark from the center. It represents the **1.5-mile abeam** position.
* **The Goal:** On the downwind leg, keep the vertical line pinned exactly on that **outermost hash mark**. If it drifts toward the center, you are too close; if it drifts away, you are too wide.



---

### 2. Coordination: Stick and Rudder as a Team
For two years, you’ve been skidding; now you’re going to carve. The Tomcat is a "long" aircraft that suffers from adverse yaw.
* **The Command:** **Left stick, left rudder / Right stick, right rudder.**
* **The Application:** When you initiate a bank, lead with a squeeze of rudder in the same direction. This "pulls" the nose into the turn so the aircraft rotates around its center of gravity rather than skidding its tail through the air.
* **The Result:** Coordinated turns preserve your energy and prevent the nose from hunting left and right during your final approach.

---

### 3. Trimming: Redefining the "Zero" Point
Trimming is the act of balancing the aircraft's weight against its lift at a specific Angle of Attack (AOA).
* **What it actually is:** You are moving the horizontal stabilizers so they naturally hold the nose up at $15$ units of AOA. 
* **How to use the Trim Hat:** 1. Once below 250 knots, drop gear and flaps. The nose will get heavy. 
    2. Do not "hold" the nose up with your arm. Instead, **click the trim hat aft (nose up)** in short pulses.
    3. Look at your AOA Indexer. You are finished when the **Amber Donut** is solid and the "E-Bracket" in the HUD is centered on your flight path.
* **The Goal:** You should be able to let go of the stick entirely and have the plane maintain its pitch.



---

### 4. The Turn to Final (The 180)
When the ship's stern passes your left shoulder, roll into a $30^{\circ}$ left bank.
* **To counteract nose drop in a bank, add a "bump" of throttle.** Banking causes a loss of vertical lift. Since you are already trimmed for AOA, do not pull the stick. Instead, push the throttles forward slightly to "catch" the nose and hold your altitude.
* **To control your pitch and rate of descent, use your throttles.** * **Add Power:** The nose rises, you climb or slow your sink.
    * **Reduce Power:** The nose settles, you sink faster.
* **Explain rudder usage on the bank:** Use a steady squeeze of left rudder to keep the turn coordinated. If the HSI vertical line is centering too slowly, the rudder helps "point" the nose toward the boat without needing a steeper bank.

---

### 5. The Groove and Touchdown
As the vertical line centers on the HSI, roll wings-level. You have 15 seconds to touchdown.
* **The Scan:** Meatball (Altitude), HSI Centerline (Lineup), Amber Donut (AOA).
* **The Touchdown:** Do not flare. Let the aircraft impact the deck at roughly 700 feet per minute. 
* **Safety:** Slam the throttles to **MIL power** (Full) the instant your wheels touch. If you catch a wire, the ship will stop you. If you miss, you’ll have the thrust to fly off the deck safely.
